# Noise analysis and PCB conception 




To identify the amount of noise generated by the motor, a noise analysis experiment took place the following step were taken :

- Lift the MTT
- Wrap a twisted pair of wires inside the motor compartement
- Attach one end of each wire to an oscilloscoipe probe and the other end to it's reference clip
- Run the motor at variable speed capture a snapshot of the noise and make and activate FFt on the Math menu

## Here are the results:

We can clearly observe the first peak on the frequency axis as 17kHz which I is due to the switching of the PWM control by the KELLY controller. We can also see (Cursor B) that it's amplitude is around -20dBV (20log(signal_volts / 1V)). The following peak in the image can be assumed to be the following harmonics generating the PWM signal. The overshoot oscillation seen on the yellow trace were identified as 1.5MHz and are of no concern to our filtering considering their high frequency.

## Sallen Key filter devellopement


To lower the impact of the PWM's noise on our steering data, we need to ceonceive a lowpasss filter to acheive enough attenuation, the pass band limit was established as 500 Hz (1kHz sampling frequency on STM32). Again choosing a 2nd order filter for simplicity would mean with this critical frequency value that our attenuation of the main noise component would be at least over 60db or 1/1000 which is largely enough.
